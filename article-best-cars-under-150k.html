Before You Buy Any Used Car in SA — Do These 5 Things

  • 01Run a VIN check on e-NaTIS to confirm the car isn't stolen or finance-encumbered. It costs around R70 and can save you from a nightmare.
  • 02Get an independent pre-purchase inspection from an AA-approved mechanic — not the seller's mechanic. Budget R800–R1,500 and it's the best money you'll spend.
  • 03Check service history stamps against the claimed mileage. If stamps don't line up or pages are missing, walk away.
  • 04Search the same car on AutoTrader and Cars.co.za before negotiating. Know the real market price — not what the seller tells you.
  • 05Test drive for at least 20 minutes including highway speeds and sharp braking. A car that drives fine at 40km/h can reveal issues at 100km/h.

Our Verdict

If we had R150,000 to spend on a used car in SA today, we'd be hunting for a clean Toyota Yaris 1.5 or Honda Jazz with full service history and under 100,000km. Both tick every box: reliability, parts availability, running costs, and long-term value.

If you want something a bit more fun, the Ford Fiesta EcoBoost is worth the extra care in buying. And if budget is the main concern, the Hyundai i20 delivers spec and reliability that most people don't give it enough credit for.

Whatever you buy — get the inspection done. A R1,000 inspection can save you a R30,000 mistake. That's not optional.
